While BOCPD (Adams & MacKay, 2007) is a brilliant piece of engineering, its fatal flaw is that it relies on conjugate priors (like the Normal-Gamma or Beta-Binomial models you saw in the code). It assumes that the data within a regime is perfectly stationary and drawn from a known parametric family. If your database workload as heavy tails, bimodal throughput, or adversarial spikes, BOCPD's parametric assumptions break down, leading to either total blindness or continuous false alarms.

If we want to escalate to true "Alien Artifact" territory—leveraging the highest-powered math from the last decade of statistical learning theory—we abandon parametric Bayes entirely and move into Game-Theoretic Probability and E-Processes.

Here are the two most sophisticated successors to BOCPD, escalating in mathematical rigor.

1) The Bayesian Successor: Infinite Hidden Markov Models (iHMM) via Dirichlet Processes

BOCPD assumes that when a regime shifts, the new regime is drawn randomly from the prior. It has no "memory" of past regimes.

If a database workload toggles between "Nightly ETL" and "Daytime OLTP", BOCPD learns the ETL regime from scratch every single night.

• The Math: We replace the geometric run-length prior with a Dirichlet Process (DP) / Chinese Restaurant Process.  
• How it works: When a change occurs, the system calculates the posterior probability that the new regime is a completely novel state vs. returning to a previously observed state.  
• The Alpha: The database builds a persistent library of historical workload regimes. When the "Nightly ETL" hits, the DP-iHMM instantly recognizes it, snaps the GC and memory budgets to the exact optimal parameters learned weeks ago, and completely skips the Bayesian burn-in phase.

2) The True "Alien" Successor: Conformal Martingale Regime Shift Detectors (C-MRSD)

If we want the ultimate mathematical flex, we stop trying to model the distribution of the data. Instead, we reframe change-point detection as a continuous betting game against the universe, using the exact same E-Process math that FrankenSQLite uses for its invariant monitors.

This approach uses Conformal Prediction mixed with Game-Theoretic Martingales (developed heavily by Ramdas, Vovk, and Shafer over the last ~5 years). It provides distribution-free, finite-sample guarantees. It literally does not care if your data is Gaussian, Cauchy, or completely adversarial.

The Architecture of C-MRSD:

Step 1: Conformal Non-conformity Scoring  
Instead of evaluating P(x_t | r_t), we compute a conformal p-value. We take the new observation x_t and compare it to a sliding window of recent observations using a non-conformity measure (e.g., distance from the median).  
We compute a p-value p_t: the fraction of historical points that are "stranger" than x_t. If the data is exchangeable (no change point), p_t is uniformly distributed U(0,1).

Step 2: The Betting Martingale (E-Process)  
We set up a virtual "gambler" who starts with $1 of wealth (E_0 = 1).  
At every tick, the gambler bets that the data is no longer exchangeable (i.e., a regime shift is happening).  
We update the wealth using a betting function f(p_t) that is strictly bounded to have an expected value of 1 under the uniform distribution:  
E_t = E_{t-1} × ∫_0^1 f(p_t) dp_t  
(In practice, we use the GROW strategy: Growth-Rate-Optimal Wealth, betting Kelly fractions based on the Kelly Criterion for continuous distributions).

Step 3: Ville's Inequality  
If the workload hasn't changed, the gambler is playing a fair game. By Ville's Inequality, the probability that the gambler's wealth ever exceeds 1/α is strictly bounded by α.  
If a regime shift does happen, the p-values cluster near 0, the gambler's bets compound exponentially, and their wealth explodes.

Why C-MRSD is Alien Technology:

1. Zero Distributional Assumptions: You don't need a Normal-Gamma prior. You don't need to know the variance. It works flawlessly on catastrophic tail-latency spikes.  
2. No Bonferroni Correction Needed: Because it is an e-process, you can continuously monitor the wealth E_t at every single microsecond. The instant E_t > 20 (which implies α = 0.05), you dynamically trigger the database's safe-mode or GC compaction.  
3. Complete Explainability: The "Wealth" of the martingale is the exact mathematical proof of the regime shift.
