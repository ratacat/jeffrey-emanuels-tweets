7) Prompt:

7. Sum of Binomial Coefficients as Vertices of a Hypercube (3D case)

Identity: \displaystyle \sum_{k=0}^n \binom{n}{k} = 2^n (illustrated with n=3)

Core idea

Each vertex of a cube corresponds to a subset of a 3‑element set. Edges connect vertices differing in exactly one element. The count of vertices at each “level” of Hamming weight equals the binomial coefficients; total vertices are 2^3=8.

Image description

Overall layout

* Single large central 3D wireframe cube drawn in perspective (not isometric, more “3D graph” style).
* Background white, cube edges thin dark gray.
* Each vertex is a colored sphere with a binary label.

Vertices and labels

* The cube’s vertices are labeled by triples of bits (0 or 1) corresponding to inclusion of elements A,B,C.
* Front-bottom-left vertex: “000”.
* Moving in x, y, z directions toggles bits. For example:
    * Front-bottom-right: “100”
    * Front-top-left: “010”
    * Back-bottom-left: “001”
    * etc., so that each axis corresponds to one element:
        x-axis → A, y-axis → B, z-axis → C.
* Each vertex sphere color encodes Hamming weight (number of 1s in the label):
    * weight 0: dark gray (the empty set).
    * weight 1: blue.
    * weight 2: green.
    * weight 3: red.

Grouping / highlighting

* Surround each group of vertices of the same weight with a faint transparent hull or dashed contour in matching color:
    * A blue translucent triangle around the three vertices of weight 1.
    * A green translucent triangle (or quadrilateral) around the three vertices of weight 2.
* Next to each group, place a label:
    * For weight 0: “\binom{3}{0} = 1 subset”.
    * For weight 1: “\binom{3}{1} = 3 subsets”.
    * For weight 2: “\binom{3}{2} = 3 subsets”.
    * For weight 3: “\binom{3}{3} = 1 subset”.

Subsets text

* Under the cube, write a small legend:
    * “bit 1 = element included, bit 0 = not included”.
    * For example:
        “010 → {B}”, “101 → {A,C}”, “111 → {A,B,C}”.

Final equation

* At the bottom, center the equation:
    \binom{3}{0} + \binom{3}{1} + \binom{3}{2} + \binom{3}{3}
    = 1 + 3 + 3 + 1
    = 8 = 2^3.
* Above the cube, small caption: “Each vertex of a 3D cube = one subset of {A,B,C}”.

The spatial grouping by Hamming weight visually shows the familiar binomial row 1–3–3–1 summing to the total number of vertices 2^3.
