And I’m sure people will say “but I tested it and it worked well!”

When you look closely, the tests are literally always some totally trivial thing where something just like it is included in the training set in a dozen different versions. 

That’s probably ok if you’re making a tic-tac-toe-as-a-service app or similarly derivative CRUD app (and sure, you can still make useful software by adhering to totally formulaic patterns). 

But the second you want to veer into something more challenging, original, or innovative, you’re going to see the difference. 

I paid for a month of the highest tier of the Kimi service and used it about twice before giving up in disgust. I suppose it could be a good choice if you simply can’t afford Claude or GPT, but it’s like comparing instant coffee to freshly ground beans.

I’m surprised that a $50b+ company is doing this sort of thing. They can’t afford to actually train a model from scratch themselves? It’s sort of shameful, really.
