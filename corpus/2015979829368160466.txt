A lot of stuff with computers that "just works" when you're doing normal things starts to break down when you push the number of concurrent agents. 

When you get past 50 agents on the same machine, even with a ton of CPU cores and RAM, things start really falling apart. 

I've been really suffering recently with very laggy ssh sessions using WezTerm's built-in version of tmux-like functionality, despite the machines having 512gb and 256gb of RAM, respectively. 

Finally, in frustration today, I did what I should have done weeks ago: find out whether it's possible to trade a bunch of RAM for better muxing performance with WezTerm. 

And yes, it's very possible and makes a huge difference. I wrote up my findings here along with a curl-bash one liner script that just does it automatically for you:

https://t.co/7NIfjF8g08
