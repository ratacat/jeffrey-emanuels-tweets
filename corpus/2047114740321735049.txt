1) Prompt:

1. Odd Numbers Build Perfect Squares

Identity: \displaystyle 1 + 3 + 5 + \dots + (2n-1) = n^2

Core idea

Each odd number is an L‑shaped “border” of unit squares added around a growing square. After n steps, you literally see an n \times n square.

Image description

Overall layout

* Single square image, white background.
* Centered is a large 6 \times 6 grid (so you can show up to n=6); grid lines are thin, light gray.
* Perspective is perfectly top-down (no 3D), like a blueprint.
* Underneath, centered text in a clean sans-serif:
    “1 + 3 + 5 + 7 + 9 + 11 = 6^2”

Color scheme

* Use a distinct saturated color for each odd term:
    * 1 → bright red
    * 3 → orange
    * 5 → yellow‑green
    * 7 → cyan
    * 9 → royal blue
    * 11 → violet
* All unfilled grid squares remain white.

Structure

* The entire 6 \times 6 square is filled by colored unit squares arranged in concentric L‑shapes:
    1. Step 1 (1):
        * A single red unit square at the top‑left corner of the grid (row 1, column 1).
    2. Step 2 (add 3):
        * Three orange squares forming an L around the red square:
            * One to the right of the red square.
            * One directly below the red square.
            * One diagonally down-right from the red square, completing a 2\times2 square block (made of 1 red + 3 orange).
    3. Step 3 (add 5):
        * Five yellow‑green squares forming a thicker L around the existing 2\times2 block:
            * Two squares extending the block to the right (so total width becomes 3).
            * Two squares extending downward (height 3).
            * One square placed at the new bottom-right corner, making a 3 \times 3 filled square.
    4. Step 4 (add 7):
        * Seven cyan squares around the 3\times3 block, again as an L:
            * Three additional squares expanding to the right (width 4).
            * Three additional squares expanding downward (height 4).
            * One at the new bottom-right to form the 4 \times 4 filled square.
    5. Step 5 (add 9):
        * Nine royal blue squares forming the L around the 4\times4.
    6. Step 6 (add 11):
        * Eleven violet squares forming the outermost L, finally filling the entire 6 \times 6 grid.

Annotations

* On the left margin, vertically centered next to each “layer,” place small labels:
    * Just outside the red zone: “1”
    * Just outside the orange L: “+ 3”
    * Then “+ 5”, “+ 7”, “+ 9”, “+ 11”, aligned with the approximate center height of each newly added L.
* In the bottom‑right corner inside the grid, in white text overlay on top of the violet area: “6^2”, slightly bold so it’s clearly visible.

The visual effect: you see a perfect 6×6 square whose area is decomposed into colored L‑shapes of lengths 1,3,5,7,9,11, making the identity obvious.
