There’s a well-documented side effect of GLP1 drugs like Ozempic that people develop an intense interest in fragrances and colognes, wanting to compulsively collect new and different pleasant smells.

I was idly wondering today about why that might be, and it suddenly dawned on me. I suspect it’s closely connected to the experience everyone has had: when you’re extremely hungry because you haven’t eaten in a long time and the smell of food seems particularly alluring.

Basically, when your body is in extreme calorie deficit, there’s some evolutionary adaptation that attempts to induce you to eat by making palatable food smells irresistibly attractive to you. 

But because of how the sense of smell works (with a bunch of different molecule receptors in your nose), it’s not just food specific, but rather includes all manner of smells that we find appealing.

And people taking GLP1 drugs tend to be in constant calorie deficit, at least in the early weeks of starting, so their smell receptors are on heightened alert.

Because they’re artificially prevented from feeling hungry, smell becomes one of the main manifestations of this biological state.

So they smell some cologne and are mesmerized by how great it smells, and think to themselves “My god, I want more of this!” 

But smell is one of those things where more doesn’t mean better, so instead they seek out variety, and start ordering lots of small bottles (“decants”) of high quality, expensive fragrances.
