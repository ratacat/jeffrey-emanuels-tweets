Claude Opus 4.1 is the most incredible tutor for self-directed learning. Because it's really N tutors at once.

To replicate the conversation I just had (excerpts shown in screenshots) with actual human tutors, you would likely need to recruit university professors or the equivalent in:

1) History/Philosophy, to know random biographical details of Friedrich Heinrich Jacobi (who popularized the concept of nihilism).

2) History of Science, to know details of Carl Gustav Jacob Jacobi (the great mathematician) and his work and life.

3) Physics/Math, to actually be able to explain CGJ Jacobi's work in detail (and it's questionable whether most could really explain it as nicely and clearly as Claude did here).

Plus a skilled frontend developer to work with the physics/math prof to create the slick visualization of the Hamilton-Jacobi Transformation applied to a pendulum. 

Basically, unless you're a centimillionaire or billionaire with scores of academics on call to tutor you, you would have a tough time learning this stuff in an interactive way like this, where you can simply ask questions and get perfectly tailored responses, with human interlocutors.

Or you can pay $20/month for access to Claude.

I have random, wandering conversations like this on a daily basis, where I start with one random question and then keep going as I think of other things I want to understand better.

Five years ago, this resulted in a dozen or more Wikipedia tabs open in Chrome as I went down random paths. But this is far more engaging and interactive because it's an actual discussion that you're a participant in.
