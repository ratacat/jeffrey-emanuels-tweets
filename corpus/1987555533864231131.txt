So why and how does any of this work? This is the core insight behind the field of crystallography: 

A crystal's diffraction pattern is the Fourier transform of its electron density.

When X-rays scatter off the regularly repeating atoms in a crystal, they create a diffraction pattern of spots whose positions and intensities encode the 3D structure in reciprocal (Fourier) space. Each spot represents a specific spatial frequency component of the electron density.

Then your task becomes inverting this frequency space representation to recover the real spatial structure.
