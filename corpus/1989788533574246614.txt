How can one reconcile this description of Bohr with the common sense notion that IQ and crystallized intelligence is closely correlated with speed of thought and reaction time, to the point where young children’s intelligence is often measured using that sort of thing (Raven’s Matrices and so on)? 

His brain must have worked very differently than most. It’s almost like an engine on a very low gear that goes very slowly, but with extreme torque so it never gets stuck in the mud.
