I came across these antique drafting instruments on eBay and bought this set for only $12. They just arrived and are fantastically well-made artifacts. 

It’s obvious that these were expensive, specialized tools for a professional that must have cost a lot of money originally.

I asked ChatGPT about it, and it estimated that the kit probably sold in the 1940s for between $185 and $460 in inflation-adjusted terms!

That got me thinking about what other products have undergone a similar collapse in relative valuation. 

There aren’t many things like that. Probably the most obvious are old upright pianos, which used to cost a ton, but which can now be had for a song on Facebook Marketplace. 

That makes some sense, because the costs are dominated by moving and tuning. 

Another example: old encyclopedia sets, which makes sense because of the internet.

Vintage fur coats are another, mostly because of changing tastes and views around animal cruelty.

In short, most of them are pretty impractical today.

But these tools are just nice to touch, and kids can use them to draw circles and other shapes and learn about geometry. And they are handy for woodworking.

You can’t really find anything else like it for $12 in terms of quality workmanship, and yet eBay is filled with them for next to nothing. Highly recommended.
