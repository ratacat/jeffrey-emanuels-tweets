Running big agent swarms can be hazardous to your machine's health (pic related). 

It has become such a problem for me that I've been forced to create a suite of programs to help me cope with this, including:

1) remote_compilation_helper (rch) for offloading builds to a fleet of dedicated VPS machines (this is the single most critical one).

2) storage_ballast_helper (sbh) for intelligently clearing out junk so you avoid filling up your primary machine or the compilation helper machines. Without this, rch stops working after a few hours. 

3) process_triage (pt) for helping to diagnose messed up runaway or zombie processes.

And I've also created various skills for working with all these tools intelligently (the ones for rch and sbh are bundled with the tools by the installers).

Without these programs, I simply wouldn't be able to work on so many complex Rust projects at the same time. 

I know that these projects don't really resonate with most developers because they simply don't face these kinds of issues and don't see the point. 

But I suspect that they increasingly will, just as I have. Because these problems are emergent as the number of agents increases, and that seems like the obvious trajectory we're on now.
