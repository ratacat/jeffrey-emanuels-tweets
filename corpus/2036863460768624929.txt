One very big and hard-to-solve problem with agent memory is that, even if you can manage to correctly and quickly identify that a memory from a past interaction is in fact relevant to the current interaction, that’s still not enough!

In the case of a rapidly-evolving codebase, for example, things that were true about it last week might be totally irrelevant today because the system has changed: bugs have been fixed, APIs revised, even the core approach might be new.

And making that determination requires knowledge and understanding of the projects (and their evolution) that the memories reference.

Basically, yet another version of:

“It ain't what you don't know that gets you into trouble. It's what you know for sure that just ain't so.”
Mark Twain
